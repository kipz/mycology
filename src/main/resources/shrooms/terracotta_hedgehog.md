### Terracotta Hedgehog
*Hydnum rufescens*

**Quick Reference:**
- **Item ID:** `mycology:terracotta_hedgehog`
- **Edibility:** Choice Edible
- **Toxicity:** None
- **Season:** Summer to autumn (August-November)
- **Habitat:** Woodland, mycorrhizal with pine, spruce, beech, oak

**In-Game Properties:**
- **Nutrition:** 5
- **Saturation:** 0.7
- **Effects:** None

**Identification:**
The Terracotta Hedgehog is a choice edible mushroom named for its terracotta/orange-brown color and spiny undersurface. The cap is 3-8cm across, terracotta to orange-brown in color, becoming lighter towards the edge. The cap has an almost 'woolly' or velvety texture and is irregular in shape, often with an uneven surface.

**Critical identifying feature:** Like other hedgehog mushrooms, it has spines/teeth hanging from the undersurface of the cap instead of gills or pores. These spines are off-white to pale pink in color, quite delicate and brittle.

The stem is quite short and stout, roughly central, 3-6cm tall, 1-2cm thick, off-white to pale pink, smooth or slightly scurfy. The flesh is white to pale, firm but brittle, with a mild pleasant smell.

An easy-to-identify mushroom with few lookalikes. Common in UK coniferous and mixed woodlands. Forms mycorrhizal associations with pine, spruce, beech, and oak trees.

**Culinary Value:**
Terracotta Hedgehogs are a popular edible species and should be picked while young and free from worms and grubs. They retain their firm texture when cooked and have a nutty, slightly spicy flavor. They work well in most mushroom dishes and are excellent as a standalone mushroom or feature ingredient. The firm texture holds up well to various cooking methods.

There are no known hazards at time of writing - this is a safe mushroom for beginners.

**Preparation:**
The spines should be brushed clean. Some people prefer to remove the spines before cooking, though this isn't necessary. The mushrooms can be saut√©ed, fried, added to stews, or preserved by drying.

**Similar Species:**
- **Wood Hedgehog** (*Hydnum repandum*) - EDIBLE. Larger, cream-colored (not terracotta), same spiny undersurface
- No poisonous lookalikes exist in UK

**Key Distinction:** The terracotta/orange color combined with the spiny undersurface makes this mushroom unmistakable. If you find a light-colored mushroom growing from the ground with a stem and spines (not gills or pores), it's a hedgehog mushroom - and they're all edible. The terracotta color distinguishes this species from the larger, cream-colored Wood Hedgehog.

**Beginner-Friendly:**
This is an excellent mushroom for novice foragers. The combination of distinctive features (terracotta color, spiny undersurface) and lack of poisonous lookalikes makes it a safe choice for those learning to forage.
