### Russet Toughshank
*Gymnopus dryophilus*

**Quick Reference:**
- **Item ID:** `mycology:spotted_toughshank`
- **Edibility:** Edible (poor quality)
- **Toxicity:** None
- **Season:** Spring to autumn (May-December)
- **Habitat:** Woodland floor

**In-Game Properties:**
- **Nutrition:** 1
- **Saturation:** 0.2
- **Effects:** None

**Identification:**
Very common small woodland mushroom. Cap 2-6cm, convex, reddish-brown to orange-brown, smooth. Gills cream, crowded. Stem slender, tough, paler than cap, hollow. Extremely abundant in deciduous woodland.

Edible but poor quality - thin flesh, tough stem, bland flavor.

### Spotted Toughshank
*Rhodocollybia maculata*

**Quick Reference:**
- **Item ID:** `mycology:spotted_toughshank`
- **Edibility:** Inedible
- **Toxicity:** None
- **Season:** Summer to autumn (June-November)
- **Habitat:** Woodland

**In-Game Properties:**
- **Nutrition:** N/A
- **Saturation:** N/A
- **Effects:** None

**Identification:**
Medium mushroom developing rusty spots with age. Cap 5-10cm, white to cream developing reddish-brown spots. Gills white, crowded, spotted. Stem white, tough, staining. Flesh white, firm.

Inedible - tough texture, bitter taste.
