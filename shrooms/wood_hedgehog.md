### Wood Hedgehog
*Hydnum repandum*

**Quick Reference:**
- **Item ID:** `mycology:wood_hedgehog`
- **Edibility:** Choice Edible
- **Toxicity:** None
- **Season:** Late summer to late autumn (August-November)
- **Habitat:** Mixed and deciduous woodlands

**In-Game Properties:**
- **Nutrition:** 6
- **Saturation:** 0.8
- **Effects:** None

**Identification:**
The Wood Hedgehog is a wonderful edible mushroom with a cap 4-15cm across. The cap is convex when young, becoming irregular and often depressed in the middle with age. The color is creamy yellow to pale flesh/salmon pink. The cap can be uneven with an undulating surface.

**Critical identifying feature:** The undersurface is covered with spines or teeth instead of gills or pores - this makes it a member of the "tooth fungi" group. The spines are cream to pale buff colored, brittle, and hang down from the underside of the cap. They are typically 2-5mm long.

The stem is 3-8cm tall, 1.5-3cm thick, often off-center or eccentric, colored similarly to the cap or paler, smooth, and solid. The flesh is thick, white, firm, and brittle. It bruises yellow to orange-brown.

**Safety Feature for Beginners:** The Hedgehog Fungus is considered a very safe mushroom for novice foragers. **If you find a light-colored mushroom growing from the ground with a stem and spines (teeth) instead of gills, it can't be anything else but a hedgehog mushroom - and they're all edible.**

Common over much of the UK, with Scotland, Wales, the north, south-west, and south-east of England as strongholds. Grows with both coniferous and deciduous trees, generally found growing with Beech. Forms mycorrhizal associations with various trees. Fruits from late summer through late autumn.

**Culinary Value:**
Wood Hedgehogs are prized for their firm, meaty texture and excellent, sweet, nutty flavor with a crunchy texture. They hold up well to cooking and are commonly sold in European markets. **Note on Maturity:** Mature specimens can develop a slightly bitter taste, so younger specimens are preferred. The bitterness is not harmful, just less pleasant.

**Similar Species:**
- **Terracotta Hedgehog** (*Hydnum rufescens*) - EDIBLE. Smaller, terracotta/orange-brown color, otherwise similar
- No poisonous lookalikes in UK

**Key Distinction:** The combination of pale cream/salmon color, spiny undersurface (not gills or pores), and growth from soil (not wood) make this unmistakable. The spines are the key - if it has downward-hanging spines and grows from the ground, it's a hedgehog mushroom.

**Excellent for Beginners:** This is one of the safest mushrooms for novice foragers due to its distinctive features and lack of poisonous lookalikes.
